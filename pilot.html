The Investor Conversation

The Pitch

This is not just a single-location business. It has real potential to become a multi-location brand. The value sits not only in the physical centre, but in the concept, the brand, and the operating model. This page addresses the key strategic points for any investor conversation.

Point 1 -- Equity & Ownership

Structuring the Deal

This is a brand with multi-location potential, not just a single asset. The equity conversation should reflect that reality. We propose two paths depending on the investor's preference.

Option A -- Balanced Structure

70-80% Investor / 20-30% Founder

A more balanced equity split that reflects both the capital contribution and the intellectual property, concept development, and operational expertise brought by the founding team. This structure keeps incentives aligned and ensures the founder retains meaningful skin in the game across future expansion.

  • Investor provides 100% of CAPEX + working capital
  • Founder/team retains 20-30% equity in the operating entity
  • Founder's share vests over 3-4 years to protect the investor
  • Aligns incentives for multi-location growth
Option B -- Capital-Heavy Structure

90% Investor / 10% Founder

If the investor strongly prefers a 90% stake, this must be clearly limited to this specific Barcelona location only. In exchange, the founder retains full ownership of the brand, concept, IP, and the right to expand independently into future locations and markets.

  • 90% equity applies to Barcelona entity only
  • Brand, concept, and IP remain 100% with Elateve
  • Founder retains rights to expand to other cities independently
  • Management agreement governs the operating relationship
Key principle: The investor is funding and owning the asset -- but the brand and platform have broader expansion potential that should not be given away with the first location. Protecting this distinction is critical for long-term value creation for both parties.
Point 2 -- Brand & IP Ownership

The Real Long-Term Value

The physical centre generates revenue. But the real scalable value sits in the brand, the methodology, and the repeatable system. This is what turns a single location into a platform.

The Biohacking Studio Brand

The name, visual identity, and market positioning. This is what guests recognise, what hotels partner with, and what scales to new cities. Owned 100% by Elateve.

The Diagnostic-Led Model

The "4 Pillars" methodology, the diagnostic-first approach, and the personalised protocol system. This is the intellectual property that differentiates Biohacking Studio from every spa and wellness centre.

The Repeatable System

The operating manual, the member app, the supplier relationships, the medical compliance framework. Everything needed to replicate this centre in Madrid, London, Dubai, or any premium city.

The Anchor Point

The investor is funding and owning the asset. The brand and platform have broader expansion potential that belongs to the concept creator.

Point 3 -- Talent & Incentives

Building a Team That Stays

This model depends heavily on quality staff and member experience. To make it scalable and defensible, the team structure must include performance-based incentives that attract and retain top talent.

01

Medical Director

The clinical anchor. Oversees all diagnostic protocols, IV treatments, and medical compliance. Compensated with a base salary plus a performance bonus tied to member retention and clinical outcomes.

02

Operations Lead

Runs the day-to-day. Member experience, scheduling, facility management, team coordination. Eligible for phantom shares or a profit-sharing bonus that vests over time -- aligned with the centre's performance.

03

Growth & Membership

Drives acquisition, partnerships, and community. Commission-based compensation tied to new member sign-ups and hotel referral revenue. The better they perform, the more they earn -- fully aligned with investor returns.

Why this matters to the investor: Performance-based incentives show long-term thinking about execution and retention. Investors see this as a positive signal -- it means the founding team is building a real business, not just filling seats. The right incentive structure reduces key-person risk and makes the model replicable across future locations.
Point 4 -- Hotel Strategy

The Hotel Opportunity

There is a compelling opportunity to position Biohacking Studio alongside premium hotels -- not as a dependency, but as a strategic partnership that accelerates growth.

The Upside
  • Immediate access to high-value clients (hotel guests are pre-qualified)
  • Lower customer acquisition cost than any digital channel
  • Strong alignment with the "longevity retreat" positioning
  • Hotels gain a premium wellness referral without operational burden
  • Referral commissions create a win-win revenue stream
The Boundaries
  • -- This is a strategic partnership, not full dependency
  • -- We retain full control of the brand identity
  • -- We maintain the direct relationship with members
  • -- The centre operates independently regardless of hotel volume
  • -- Hotel referrals are a growth accelerator, not a lifeline
Framing for the conversation: Hotels are a powerful acquisition channel that we layer on top of a standalone membership model. The centre is viable with zero hotel referrals. Hotel partnerships make it exceptional. This hybrid model gives us the best of both worlds -- independence with acceleration.
